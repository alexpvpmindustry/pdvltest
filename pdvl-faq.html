PDVL FAQ

If you're considering applying for a Private Hire Car Driver's Vocational Licence (PDVL) in Singapore, you may have some questions about the application process, fees, eligibility requirements, and driving regulations. Here are answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about PDVL licensing:

What are the eligibility requirements for the PDVL?

To be eligible for a PDVL in Singapore, you must:

  • Be at least 30 years old
  • Hold a valid Class 3/3A driving licence for at least 1 year
  • Pass the PDVL test
  • Complete the 10-hour PDVL training course
  • Undergo a medical check-up
  • Submit the PDVL application form and fee

How do I apply for the PDVL?

You can apply for the PDVL online or in person at the Land Transport Authority (LTA) office. You'll need to provide documentation such as your identification card or passport, driving licence, and proof of completion of the PDVL training course.

How long does it take to process a PDVL application?

The processing time for a PDVL application is typically 3 to 4 weeks.

What is the PDVL test like?

The PDVL test consists of a theory test X3. The theory test covers topics such as driving regulations and safety.

What is the passing score for the PDVL test?

The passing score for the PDVL test is 80% for the test.

How often do I need to renew my PDVL?

Your PDVL is valid for 3 years from the date of issue. You'll need to renew your PDVL before it expires to continue driving.

What is the renewal fee for the PDVL?

The renewal fee for the PDVL is currently S$40.

What documentation do I need to provide for PDVL renewal?

When you renew your PDVL, you'll need to provide a recent passport-sized photograph, a copy of your identification card or passport, your current PDVL, and proof of completion of the 10-hour PDVL refresher course (if your PDVL is expiring for the first time after 1 July 2020).

Can I drive with a foreign driving licence if I have a PDVL?

No, you must hold a valid Singapore driving licence (Class 3/3A) to drive with a PDVL.

What are the regulations for private hire car drivers in Singapore?

Private hire car drivers in Singapore must meet safety requirements, display a tamper-evident decal on the front and back windscreens of the vehicle, and have commercial motor insurance. They are also only allowed to provide pre-booked private hire car services.

What happens if I fail the PDVL test?

If you fail the PDVL test, you can retake the test after booking a new session.
