PDVL VS TDVL

If you're interested in driving a taxi or a private hire car in Singapore, you'll need to obtain a license. There are two types of licenses available: Private Hire Car Driver's Vocational Licence (PDVL) and Taxi Driver's Vocational Licence (TDVL). Here's a comparison of the PDVL and TDVL licenses:

Eligibility Requirements

The eligibility requirements for the PDVL and TDVL licenses are different:

  • PDVL: At least 30 years old, hold a valid Class 3/3A driving licence for at least 1 year, pass the PDVL test, complete the 10-hour PDVL training course, undergo a medical check-up, and submit the PDVL application form and fee.
  • TDVL: At least 30 years old, hold a valid Class 3/3A driving licence for at least 1 year, pass the TDVL test, undergo a medical check-up, and submit the TDVL application form and fee.

Application Process

The application processes for the PDVL and TDVL licenses are different:

  • PDVL: Apply online or in person at the Land Transport Authority (LTA) office.
  • TDVL: Apply in person at the Taxi Academy or Singapore Taxi Academy.

Driving Regulations

The driving regulations for the PDVL and TDVL licenses are different:

  • PDVL: Allowed to provide pre-booked private hire car services only, must display a tamper-evident decal on the front and back windscreens of the vehicle, must meet safety requirements, and must have commercial motor insurance.
  • TDVL: Allowed to provide street-hail taxi services and pre-booked taxi services, must display a taxi sign on the roof of the vehicle, must meet safety requirements, and must have commercial motor insurance.

By understanding the differences between the PDVL and TDVL licenses, you can choose the license that best fits your needs and qualifications.
